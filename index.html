ANTONYMS and SYNONYMS


In a nutshell the poem ‘if’ is:

A handbook to living a principled and full life, providing advice on how to confront problems, make smart decisions, and keep one's integrity in the face of adversity.

Words we learnt from the poem ‘If’ by Rudyard Kipling:

  1. Knave: a dishonest or unscrupulous person
    Use: Stay away from knaves even if you do not have any friends.
  2. Impostor: a person who pretends to be someone else in order to deceive others, especially for fraudulent gain.
    Use: Impostors do not have a constant personality.
